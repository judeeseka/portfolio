## 🔥 PORTFOLIO WEBSITE GENERATION PROMPT

> **Role & Perspective**
> You are a senior frontend hiring manager and recruiter with 10+ years of experience reviewing portfolios for startups, scaleups, and AI/agentic companies. You are building a **single-page portfolio website** for a frontend engineer with real production experience.
> Your goal is to design and write a portfolio that:

* Signals **senior thinking**
* Communicates **real-world impact**
* Feels **professional, credible, and interview-worthy**
* Makes a hiring manager confident within **2–4 minutes of scanning**

You should be proud to recommend this engineer for interviews after reviewing the site.

---

### 🎯 Target Profile

* Frontend Engineer (React / Next.js)
* 2+ years experience
* Has worked on **production SaaS**, **multi-role platforms**, and **workflow automation**
* Experience includes being **sole frontend engineer** and **engineering lead**
* Targeting **startups, scaleups, and AI/agentic product companies**
* Portfolio must avoid junior clichés and generic statements

---

## 🧱 REQUIRED STRUCTURE (Single Page)

Design and write content for the following sections **in this exact order**, ensuring clarity, brevity, and impact.

---

### 1️⃣ Hero Section (Most Important)

**Goal:** Immediately answer:

* What does this engineer build?
* At what level?
* Why should I keep reading?

**Requirements:**

* Clear, professional headline (no fluff, no buzzwords)
* Specific specialization (React, Next.js, complex systems)
* Credibility anchor (real products, leadership, scale)
* Strong CTAs: View Projects, Download CV, GitHub

**Tone:** Confident, calm, senior — not flashy, not poetic.

---

### 2️⃣ Engineering Focus (Senior Signal Section)

**Goal:** Show how the engineer thinks, not just what they use.

**Requirements:**

* 4–6 bullet points
* Emphasize:

  * Scalable frontend architecture
  * State management strategy
  * Performance and data-heavy UIs
  * Workflow-driven and automated systems
* Avoid listing tools here

This section should make a CTO think:

> “This person understands systems.”

---

### 3️⃣ Featured Projects

**Goal:** Prove real-world experience fast.

**Requirements:**

* 3–4 projects max
* Correct ordering:

  1. Production SaaS / Enterprise tool (NDA-safe)
  2. Multi-role marketplace (leadership)
  3. Product MVP (product thinking)
  4. Optional data-heavy app

Each project preview must include:

* Project name
* One-line product description (problem-focused)
* Role (Sole FE / Frontend Lead / Contributor)
* Tech stack
* CTA: View Case Study

---

### 4️⃣ Project Case Studies (Expandable / Modal Content)

Each project must include **short but meaningful** sections:

**Overview**

* What the product is
* Who it’s for

**Problem**

* What was broken, slow, or missing

**Role & Ownership**

* Be explicit about responsibility and scope

**Key Technical Decisions**

* Architecture
* State management
* Integration patterns
* Tradeoffs

**Impact**

* Business or user value
* Scale or efficiency gained

**NDA Handling**

* For internal products, use anonymized summaries
* Explicitly label as “NDA-safe summary”

Tone should reflect **engineering judgment**, not marketing.

---

### 5️⃣ About Section (Short, Focused)

**Goal:** Humanize without storytelling fluff.

**Requirements:**

* 4–6 lines max
* Mention:

  * Years of experience
  * Types of systems built
  * Collaboration style
  * Leadership exposure
* No childhood stories, no “passion for tech” clichés

---

### 6️⃣ Technology Stack (Grouped, Senior-Friendly)

Group technologies into:

* Core Frontend
* State & Data
* UI & Styling
* Backend & Services
* Tooling

Avoid comma-separated lists.

---

### 7️⃣ Contact Section

**Goal:** Make it easy to reach out.

**Requirements:**

* Short, human intro sentence
* Simple contact form OR email + LinkedIn
* No gimmicks

---

## 🧠 Style & Tone Rules

* Avoid buzzwords like *“cutting-edge”, “passionate”, “rockstar”*
* Be specific, grounded, and calm
* Write like a senior engineer explaining work to another engineer
* Prefer clarity over cleverness
* Assume the reader is busy and skeptical

---

## 🚫 Explicitly Avoid

* Long personal essays
* Skill bars or percentages
* Over-animation
* Listing every project ever done
* Repeating the same tech stack everywhere without context

---

## ✅ Final Success Criteria

When a recruiter or CTO scans this portfolio, they should conclude:

* This engineer has **real production experience**
* They understand **systems and tradeoffs**
* They can be trusted with **complex frontend ownership**
* This is someone worth interviewing
